Learn About Environmental Pollution

Understand key environmental concepts, pollution types, and scientific terms to better interpret pollution data.

AQI stands for Air Quality Index. It is a numerical scale used to measure the quality of air and its impact on health. The higher the AQI value, the greater the level of air pollution and health concern.

  • 0–50: Good
  • 51–100: Moderate
  • 101–200: Unhealthy
  • 200+: Hazardous

PM2.5 refers to fine particulate matter that is 2.5 micrometers or smaller. These particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and even enter the bloodstream, causing respiratory and cardiovascular diseases.

🌫 Air Pollution

Caused by vehicle emissions, industrial smoke, and burning fuels.

💧 Water Pollution

Occurs due to industrial waste, sewage discharge, and plastic waste.

🌱 Soil Pollution

Caused by chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and hazardous waste.

🔊 Noise Pollution

Excessive sound levels from traffic, construction, and industries.
